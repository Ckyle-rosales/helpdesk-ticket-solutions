# Ticket 002: PC Not Booting

## Issue
User reports that their desktop computer will not power on. No display is shown when pressing the power button.

## Environment
- Custom-built desktop PC
- Windows 10
- Dedicated GPU installed

## Troubleshooting Steps
1. Verified that the power cable was securely connected to the power supply and wall outlet
2. Checked that the power supply switch was turned on
3. Observed motherboard LEDs for any signs of power
4. Reseated the RAM modules to ensure proper connection
5. Reseated the GPU and confirmed all power connectors were securely attached
6. Tested the system with a different power cable
7. Attempted to boot with minimal components (removed unnecessary peripherals)

## Findings
The system was not receiving consistent power due to a loose GPU power connector.

## Resolution
Reseated and secured the GPU power cable. System powered on successfully and booted into the operating system.

## Key Takeaways
Loose or improperly connected components, especially power connectors, can prevent a system from booting properly.

## Customer Communication
Explained the issue in simple terms and advised the user to ensure all internal connections remain secure when moving or cleaning the system.

## Additional Checks
- Verified PSU functionality
- Checked for POST beeps or error indicators
- Ensured monitor and display cables were functional
